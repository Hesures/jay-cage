Antonio Murillo

The forgotten guitarist

Time line of Antonio's life:

Birth - Antonio was born in Cordoba, Spain, at the modest neighborhood of El Naranjo.

Childhood - Since he was a child, he always was interested on Flamenco music. When his mother sended him to go shopping, he used to distract in some bars of the way to listen other guitarists playing Flamenco. He started playing the guitar when he was twelve years old, having as reference guitarists Sabicas, Melchor de Marchena and Niño Ricardo.

Adulthood - He got married with Maria de los Dolores (Loli), with whom he still remains today.

Work milestones - At twenty-five years old, he founded the academy "El bordón y la prima" (which in spanish means "The sixth and the first"), referring to the name of the sixth (bordón) and the first (prima) strings of the guitar. Since that date, Antonio has never stops teaching Flamenco guitar or either participating in dancing performances as a guitarist until his retirement.

Gigs and collaborations - Antonio played with Paco Peña, and he also played for singers like Bartolomé Castillejo or Valentín Jurado. Antonio also collaborated with a considerable number of dancers and participated in a scene in the movie "La casa de las Palomas", directed by Claudio Guerín. See the scene here.
